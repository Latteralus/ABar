After Financial Stage, wishlist items are tracked here in no specific order.

## Completed / first-pass complete

1. **Adjustable bar share of tips — Done.** The Settings screen now exposes a Bar Share slider backed by `GameState.policies.barTipSharePercent`. Payments use the policy instead of a fixed 25% bar share. Future employee-relations systems can still make staff react to tip-share changes.
2. **Adjustable attraction prices and High/Mid/Low indicators — Done.** Owned attractions have editable price-per-game fields, and menu/attraction pricing screens show Low/Mid/High indicators based on current price versus suggested/catalog price.
3. **Price dialogue — First pass done.** Customers can log dialogue when drink prices are unusually high or low, with small satisfaction effects. Future reputation work should connect these signals to popularity, reviews, and demand.
4. **Reports include employee wages — Done as part of Stage 5.** Payroll now accrues daily from employee wages and is included in daily reports/financials and weekly bill generation.
5. **Capacity / Floor View reconciliation — Done.** Live Operations now separates customer capacity, available seats, and standing/waiting capacity. Floor View renders standing/waiting slots to match total capacity and uses stable arrival-order placement so customers no longer visually jump between tables because their status changed.
6. **Expanded realistic Equipment catalog and equipment space — First pass done.** The catalog now includes upgraded bar stations, refrigerators/freezers, microwaves, fryers, griddles, ovens, draft systems, dish/glass washers, shelving, POS/security, and maintenance tools with realistic prices, `spaceUnits`, and `tier` metadata. Equipment purchases are blocked when they exceed the property's equipment-space cap, and higher-tier same-category items are labeled as upgrades.
7. **Reputation/popularity loop — Done (Master Plan Stage 6).** A persistent 0-100 service-reputation score (`GameState.reputation`) updates once per closed day from real signals (wait times, satisfaction, lost/removed customers, cleanliness, equipment problems, staff skill) with heavy damping so no single day swings it — see the new Reputation screen for score history and positive/negative factor breakdowns. Departing customers may leave a predefined-phrase-assembled review (Section 29, gated by `reviewTendency`, never an AI service) shown there and on Overview. Four advertising channels (newspaper/flyers/radio/social media) build demand gradually over several days via a delay-ramp-decline curve; three same-day promotions (drink special, happy hour, live entertainment) modify demand for one day, and the two drink-focused ones apply a real price discount at order time. Reputation and active promotions both feed `customerArrivals`' demand multiplier — advertising modifies odds, it never guarantees customers.
8. **Drink-system overhaul — Done.** 15 new inventory items (tequila, gin, triple sec, dry vermouth, fresh lime juice, simple syrup, orange/cranberry juice, sour mix, club soda, margarita salt, mint, olives, orange slice, Jell-O shot mix, and a keg-unit Draft Lager Keg — the first real use of the previously-unused `"keg"` `InventoryUnit`) and 8 new drinks (Draft Lager, Margarita, Cosmopolitan, Mojito, Screwdriver, Martini, Whiskey Sour, Jell-O Shot). Draft Lager is gated behind the `draft_system` equipment category, which finally has a real purpose (its benefit text used to say "once draft products are added"). Employee task labels now name the specific drink — `utils/taskProgress.ts` resolves the task's order to a product name, e.g. `"Making Margarita for Jamie Rivera (60%)"` instead of the old generic `"Preparing a drink for Jamie Rivera"` — confirmed live alongside end-to-end order/serve log lines for every new drink.
9. **Televisions and Big Screens — Done.** TVs turned out not to need a new domain — they're just a new `"tv"` `EquipmentCategory` (Flat-Screen/Large-Screen TV), so purchase, floor-space gating, condition decay, breakdown, and staff repair all came for free from the existing Equipment machinery. The only new code is the ambient effect itself (`simulation/engine/tvEffects.ts`): while any owned TV is operational, seated customers get a small satisfaction trickle and extra minutes added to their consuming/socializing dwell time, plus a small per-minute chance of ordering another round (reusing the exact same `take_order` pipeline Attraction participants already use). It's a binary gate (any one working TV is enough), not scaled by count or tier — a deliberate simplification.
